Welcome to Freya Rockets an Ethereum Funded initiative!

Freya is a rocket startup that seeks to develop rockets that can place satellites into orbit. Using iterative development and design.
Freya will use computer generated models for designing. 3D PRINTED metal components. Oil rig based launch platform.

Freya is an integrated satellite deployment system. It is primarily funded in ETH(ethereum) using investor pooling and voting process in the design,planning and deployment phase. Construction of components is done through building warehouses and then integrating the components from the warehouses in a single location. The complete rocket will be launched from a sea oil rig. A smart contract will be utilised to fund the project. This contract is ETH based. Models will be readily available on the site for warehouses, engines, rocket casings etc. This Contract is still in development phase and will soon be availble for download.

Ethereum Smart Contract.

Why Freya will leverage an Ethereum smart contract?

Developing a space rocket using Ethereum, a blockchain platform primarily used for smart contracts and decentralized applications. Here's how Ethereum could intersect Freya with rocket development: 1. Funding the Project through TokenizationInitial Coin Offering (ICO): Our developers will create a token on the Ethereum blockchain to fund Freya rocket project. Investors would buy these tokens, effectively contributing to the development in exchange for future rewards or shares in the project. This is solely at the risk of investors since rockets are prone to failure though this coukld be proven otherwise.

How to utilize Ethereum inAssembly and Integration?

Smart Contracts for Partnerships: Use Ethereum smart contracts to manage partnerships with aerospace suppliers or contractors, ensuring that payments and obligations are only fulfilled when certain milestones are reached, reducing risk. 3. Supply Chain and Component Tracking Blockchain for Supply Chain: Ethereum’s blockchain can be used to track the entire supply chain for rocket components. From material sourcing to final assembly, every component can be recorded immutably on the blockchain. This ensures transparency and accountability, especially for aerospace-grade components. Smart Contracts for Supply Chain: Smart contracts can automate payments or trigger actions in the supply chain, such as releasing funds when parts are delivered or certain milestones in manufacturing are achieved.
